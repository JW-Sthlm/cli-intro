🔴 DEMO 01 — The Basics

~5 min · Show Copilot CLI understands natural language, has slash commands, and can work with your screen
Setup
✓
Copilot CLI running in PowerShell
✓
Calendar and email accessible (M365 MCP configured)
1. Natural language — just talk to it
What meetings do I have today?
No special syntax. No query language. Just ask in plain English. It uses your M365 connection to check your actual calendar.
2. Slash commands — quick actions
/help
Slash commands are shortcuts for common actions. Point out: /model (switch AI models), /env (see what's connected), /plan (think before doing), /research (deep investigation mode).
/model
You can pick different AI models depending on the task. Like choosing the right tool from a toolbox.
3. @ mentions — point it at files
@README.md summarize this file
The @ symbol lets you point Copilot at specific files. It reads them and works with the content. Navigate to a folder with a README first.
4. # references — GitHub issues and PRs
#1 what is this issue about?
# connects to GitHub. You can reference issues, pull requests — anything in your repos.
5. Screenshot — what's on your screen
1
Show something on screen (a PMX dashboard, an email, a webpage)
2
Take a screenshot (Win+Shift+S or PrtSc)
Look at my last screenshot and tell me what you see
Copilot can analyze screenshots. Useful for: "What does this error mean?", "Summarize this dashboard", "Draft a response to this email I'm looking at."

Key Messages

  • You don't need to learn a programming language — it speaks yours
  • Slash commands = shortcuts, not requirements
  • It connects to your actual tools (calendar, email, files, GitHub)
  • Screenshots make it visual — show it what you see

🔴 DEMO 02 — Partner Connect Prep with PMX

~10 min · Walk through a realistic Connect preparation using PMX, MSX, M365, and screenshot tools
Scenario

"You have a partner Connect meeting tomorrow. You need to prepare: review your projects, check outcomes, look at opportunities, and draft talking points."

Setup
✓
Copilot CLI running with PMX, M365, and GitHub MCP servers loaded
✓
Pick a real partner you have projects with (or use a well-known one for demo purposes)
✓
Have an email or Teams chat related to that partner visible
1. Pull your active projects
Show me my active PMX projects
This hits Dynamics 365 live. No need to open a browser, navigate to PMX, filter views. Just ask.
2. Check outcome coverage
Which of my projects are missing outcomes?
This is one of the most common PMX hygiene issues. Instead of clicking through each project, Copilot checks them all at once. Point out: MSX Opportunity, Solution ID, Referral — the three outcome types.
3. Dive into a specific partner
Show me the team for [Partner Name] — who is PDM, PTS, PSAM?
Before a Connect, you want to know who's in what role. This pulls the partner team directly.
4. Search for related opportunities
Search MSX opportunities for [Partner Name] — show me open ones in EMEA
Now we're crossing from PMX into MSX. Copilot connects the dots between your project work and the sales pipeline. Look for gaps — opportunities without projects, or projects without linked opportunities.
5. Screenshot context
1
Open a partner email or Teams chat on screen. Take a screenshot.
Look at my last screenshot. What is the key ask or topic in this message?
Sometimes the context you need is visual — an email you received, a dashboard, a chat thread. Screenshot it, ask Copilot to summarize.
6. Draft talking points
Based on the PMX projects, MSX opportunities, and partner team you just showed me — draft 3-4 talking points for my Connect meeting with [Partner Name] tomorrow. Focus on: outcome gaps, active opportunities, and any projects that need attention.
This is the payoff. Everything we just looked at — Copilot synthesizes into actionable talking points. You could paste this into your meeting notes or email it to the partner team.
7. (Optional) Draft a prep email
Draft a short email to the PDM for [Partner Name] with the talking points we just created. Use a professional but informal tone.
You can go from data → analysis → action in one session. No tab-switching, no copy-pasting between apps.

Key Messages

  • PMX + MSX + M365 = a complete Connect prep workflow
  • Copilot connects data sources that you'd normally have to jump between manually
  • The screenshot tool adds visual context that's hard to get otherwise
  • The draft output is a starting point, not a final product — you're the curator

🔴 DEMO 03 — Power Moves

~7 min · /research for deep investigation, skills for domain expertise, combined with PMX/MSX data for strategic output
Setup
✓
Copilot CLI running with all MCP servers
✓
Pick a topic relevant to your audience (e.g., a partner's technology area, a market trend)
1. /research — deep investigation mode
/research What is the current state of AI agent adoption among ISVs in the Nordic market? Include trends, challenges, and opportunities for Microsoft partners.
/research is different from a regular question. It runs a deep search — web sources, GitHub, structured data — and produces a report with citations. Think of it as having a research analyst on your team.
⏳
Wait for research to complete (usually 30-60 seconds). Point out citations, structure, depth.
2. Combine research with PMX data
Now look at the MSX opportunities in EMEA related to AI agents or autonomous systems. Cross-reference with what you just found — are there gaps we should be pursuing?
This is where it gets powerful. You take external intelligence (market research) and overlay it with internal data (your MSX pipeline). Copilot can spot patterns you'd miss manually.
3. Generate a partner roadmap
Based on the research and MSX opportunity data, draft a suggested technical roadmap for a partner looking to build AI agent solutions on Azure. Include: which Azure services to start with, what skills they need, and 2-3 concrete project ideas with estimated complexity.
This is the kind of output you'd normally spend hours assembling. Market context + pipeline data + Azure expertise = a roadmap you can actually bring to a partner conversation.
4. Skills — domain expertise on demand
/skills
Skills are pre-packaged expertise. Show the list — highlight Azure skills (deploy, diagnostics, compliance), PMX tools, and content creation.
Use the azure-compute skill to recommend a VM size for a partner running a mid-size SQL Server workload with 32GB RAM and 8 cores.
You don't need to memorize Azure VM SKUs. Skills give Copilot access to pricing APIs, best practices, configuration guides.
5. Custom agents and extensions
/env
Show the extensions and agents section. Explain: custom agents = specialized personas, extensions = new tools, instructions files = team conventions. This is how you go from "useful tool" to "customized assistant."
6. /plan — think before you act
/plan Create a one-page partner brief for [Partner Name] that includes their project portfolio, outcome coverage, open opportunities, and a recommended next step.
/plan creates a structured plan before doing anything. You review it, adjust it, then tell Copilot to execute. Like giving a brief to a colleague.

Key Messages

  • /research = deep investigation with citations, not just a quick answer
  • Combining research + internal data (PMX/MSX) = strategic insights
  • Skills give Copilot domain expertise you don't have to carry in your head
  • Custom agents and instructions make it YOUR assistant, not a generic one
  • /plan gives you control — review before execution
